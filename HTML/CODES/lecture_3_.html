The <strong> element is used to emphasize text with strong importance, seriousness, or urgency. Unlike <b>, it conveys semantic meaning and is recognized by screen readers and search engines.

The <b> element highlights text to draw attention without giving it extra importance or changing its tone. It is often used for keywords, product names, actionable text, or an article introduction.

The <i> tag is used to style text in italics without adding semantic emphasis like <em>

This is italic text. The <em> element in HTML is used to emphasize text, typically indicating stress or importance in the content. This emphasis is also recognized by screen readers, and it often renders text in italics.

The <u> element is used to indicate text with a non-textual annotation, such as marking a misspelt word or a Chinese proper name.

The <small> element makes the text one size smaller than the parent element's font size. It is often used to represent the fine print, copyright information, or disclaimers.

The <big> element makes the text one size larger than the parent element's font size. It is often used to emphasize a section of text or other elements.
